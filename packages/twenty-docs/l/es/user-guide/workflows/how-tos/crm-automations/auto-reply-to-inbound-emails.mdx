---
title: Auto-Reply to Inbound Emails
description: Build a workflow that sends threaded replies to incoming emails automatically.
---

Send Email actions now support an **In-Reply-To** field, which lets your workflow replies land inside the original email thread. This opens up use cases like instant acknowledgments, auto-replies to leads, and AI-drafted follow-ups — all threaded naturally in the recipient's inbox.

## How email threading works

Every email carries a hidden `Message-ID` header — a unique fingerprint assigned by the sender's mail server. When you reply to an email, your mail client sets an `In-Reply-To` header referencing that fingerprint. That's how Gmail, Outlook, and every other client groups messages into threads.

In Twenty, that fingerprint is stored as `headerMessageId` on the Message object. Your workflow grabs it and passes it into the Send Email action's In-Reply-To field.

## Building the workflow

### Step 1: Create a new workflow

Head to **Settings -> Workflows** and click **+ New Workflow**.

### Step 2: Trigger on incoming messages

Pick **When a Record is Created** and choose **Messages**.

Every time an email lands in Twenty, this fires.

![Workflow overview with trigger, search records, and send email steps](/images/user-guide/workflows/auto-reply/workflow-overview.png)

### Step 3: Look up who sent it

Add a **Search Records** action. The sender's address isn't on the message itself — it's on the related `Message Participant` record.

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Object** | Message Participants |
| **Filter** | Message **is** `{{trigger.properties.after.id}}` |
| **Filter** | Role **is** From |
| **Limit** | 1 |

This gives you the sender's email in `handle`.

![Search Records config filtering Message Participants by message and role](/images/user-guide/workflows/auto-reply/search-records.png)

### Step 4: Send a threaded reply

Add a **Send Email** action. Click **Advanced options**, then **Add In-Reply-To**.

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **To** | `{{Find Sender.first.handle}}` |
| **Subject** | `Re: {{trigger.properties.after.subject}}` |
| **Body** | Whatever you'd want to say |
| **In-Reply-To** | `{{trigger.properties.after.headerMessageId}}` |

That last field is what makes it a reply instead of a new conversation.

![Send Email config with In-Reply-To set to headerMessageId](/images/user-guide/workflows/auto-reply/send-email.png)

<Tip>
**In-Reply-To** expects a `message.headerMessageId` from a previous step — it's the email's unique fingerprint, not a recipient address. If you leave it empty, the email still sends, just as a standalone message.
</Tip>

### Step 5: Test and activate

Hit **Test**, then check your email client. The reply should appear nested under the original message.

![Gmail showing the automated reply threaded under the original email](/images/user-guide/workflows/auto-reply/gmail-threaded-reply.png)

Activate when you're happy with it.

## Ideas to build on

- **Only reply to VIPs** — add a branch that checks the sender's domain or whether they exist as a Contact in Twenty
- **AI-drafted follow-ups** — add a Code action before Send Email to generate a contextual response from the message body
- **Route by subject** — use branches to handle support requests differently from sales inquiries
