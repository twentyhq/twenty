The **data model** is how you describe your business to Twenty. It consists of three ideas. Objects are the kinds of things you track. Fields are the information you keep on each one. Relations connect them. Done well, every fact about your business lives in exactly one place.

## Objects

An **object** is one kind of thing you track, comparable to one tab in a spreadsheet. A packaging supplier tracks the companies that buy from it, the people at those companies, its open deals and the work around them. Twenty ships with objects for all of these.

- **Companies**, the organizations you sell to
- **People**, the contacts at those organizations
- **Opportunities**, Twenty's term for deals
- **Tasks** and **Notes**, for the work and context around the rest

Each entry inside an object is a **record**. Harbor Foods is a record in Companies. The purchasing manager there is a record in People.

Most businesses also track something no CRM ships with. For the packaging supplier it's print runs. A print run is one batch of boxes going through the presses, with a design, a quantity, a ship date and a destination plant. For that you create a **custom object**. Custom objects behave exactly like built-in ones. They get their own section in the sidebar, their own pages and views, their own API endpoints. In another business the custom object is property listings or job applications.

## Fields

A **field** is one piece of information on a record. In spreadsheet terms, one column. A phone number, an order quantity, a plant address, the current artwork version.

Every field has a **type** that defines what the field holds, whether text, a number, a date, an amount of money or a selection from a list. The type determines what Twenty does with the value.

- **Currency** fields hold amounts Twenty does arithmetic on. A dashboard totals the value of open deals from them.
- **Select** fields hold one value from a fixed list. Deal stages are Select fields. Pipeline columns come straight from them.
- **Date** fields place records on a calendar.
- **Files** fields hold documents, so the artwork PDF lives on the record it describes.

There are more than twenty types in total. Twenty also maintains a few fields automatically on every record, including who created it and when it last changed.

## Relations

Records rarely stand alone. A person works at a company. A print run belongs to a customer. A **relation** is a field that holds another record instead of a plain value. On a contact's record, the Company field holds the Harbor Foods record itself rather than the text `Harbor Foods`.

Relations keep every fact in one place. Harbor Foods' current artwork lives on its record and every linked print run reads it from there. A rebrand becomes a one-field edit. Nothing downstream ships with a stale design.

Relations work in both directions. The Harbor Foods page lists its people, its open deals, its notes and every print run sent to it. Each of those records shows which company it belongs to.
