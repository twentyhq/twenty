The People object is one of Twenty's core objects, designed to store information about individuals you interact with in your business. This includes customers, prospects, partners, team members, and any other contacts relevant to your organization.

## Overview

### What is the People Object?
The People object represents individual contacts in your CRM. Each person record contains personal and professional information, contact details, and relationship data that helps you manage your business relationships effectively.

### Key Characteristics
- **Central to CRM operations**: Most CRM activities revolve around people
- **Email and calendar integration**: Automatically syncs with connected email and calendar accounts
- **Relationship-aware**: Links to companies, opportunities, tasks, and other objects
- **Activity tracking**: Maintains history of all interactions and communications
- **Customizable**: Supports custom fields for industry-specific or unique business needs

## Standard Fields

### Personal Information

**Name Fields**:
- **First Name**: Individual's given name
- **Last Name**: Individual's family name
- **Display Name**: Automatically generated full name for easy identification

**Contact Information**:
- **Email**: Primary email address (unique identifier)
- **Phone**: Primary phone number
- **City**: Current city of residence or work

### Professional Information

**Work Details**:
- **Job Title**: Current position or role
- **Company**: Linked to the Companies object (relation field)
- **LinkedIn URL**: Professional social media profile

### System Fields

**Metadata**:
- **Created At**: When the record was first created
- **Updated At**: When the record was last modified
- **Created By**: User who created the record
- **Position**: Used for record ordering in views

## Relationships and Connections

### Company Relationship

**One-to-Many Relationship**:
- Each person can be associated with one company
- Each company can have multiple people associated with it
- This relationship enables company-wide contact management and communication tracking

**Benefits**:
- See all contacts at a company in one place
- Track company-wide communication history
- Manage account relationships holistically

### Email and Calendar Integration

**Automatic Linking**:
- Emails to/from people are automatically linked to their records
- Calendar events with people are tracked and displayed
- Communication history provides complete interaction context

**Visibility Settings**:
- Control what email content is visible (full content, metadata only, etc.)
- Configure calendar event visibility and privacy settings
- Manage sync settings for different email folders

### Activity Tracking

**Linked Activities**:
- **Tasks**: Assigned to or related to specific people
- **Notes**: Personal observations, meeting minutes, or important details
- **Opportunities**: Sales deals associated with the person
- **Custom Activities**: Any custom objects you've created that relate to people

## Email and Communication Features

### Email Sync Integration

**Automatic Email Linking**:
- Emails are automatically matched to people based on email addresses
- Full email threads are preserved and accessible from person records
- Email history provides context for all communications

**Email Visibility Options**:
- **Full Content**: Complete email text and attachments
- **Metadata Only**: Subject, sender, recipient, and timestamp
- **Subject + Metadata**: Subject line plus basic information
- **No Sync**: Exclude specific people from email sync

### Communication History

**Comprehensive Timeline**:
- All emails sent and received
- Calendar events and meetings
- Tasks and follow-up activities
- Notes and manual entries
- System-generated activities

**Context and Insights**:
- Last contact date and method
- Communication frequency patterns
- Response times and engagement levels
- Relationship strength indicators

## Customization Options

### Custom Fields

**Common Custom Field Types**:
- **Lead Source**: How you first connected with this person
- **Industry Experience**: Their professional background
- **Interests**: Personal or professional interests
- **Preferred Communication**: Email, phone, text, etc.
- **Time Zone**: For scheduling across different regions
- **Language**: Primary language for communication

**Industry-Specific Fields**:
- **Healthcare**: License numbers, specializations, hospital affiliations
- **Real Estate**: Property interests, budget range, preferred areas
- **Education**: Institution, degree, graduation year
- **Technology**: Technical skills, programming languages, certifications

### Field Configuration

**Field Properties**:
- **Required vs. Optional**: Determine which fields must be filled
- **Unique Constraints**: Ensure no duplicate values (like email addresses)
- **Default Values**: Set standard values for new records
- **Validation Rules**: Ensure data quality and consistency

**Display Options**:
- **Field Visibility**: Control which fields appear in different views
- **Field Ordering**: Arrange fields logically for data entry
- **Conditional Display**: Show/hide fields based on other field values
- **Help Text**: Provide guidance for field completion

## Views and Organization

### Default Views

**All People**:
- Unfiltered view of all person records
- Default sorting and field display
- Starting point for most people-related activities

**Recent People**:
- Recently created or updated person records
- Helps track new contacts and recent changes
- Useful for follow-up activities

### Custom Views

**Common View Types**:
- **By Company**: Group people by their associated company
- **By Lead Source**: Organize by how contacts were acquired
- **By Geography**: Filter by city, state, or region
- **By Activity**: Show people based on recent interactions
- **By Status**: Organize by lead status, customer type, etc.

**View Customization**:
- **Filters**: Show only people meeting specific criteria
- **Sorting**: Order by name, company, last contact, etc.
- **Field Selection**: Choose which information to display
- **Grouping**: Organize people by select field values

## Data Quality and Management

### Duplicate Prevention

**Unique Identifiers**:
- Email addresses must be unique across all people
- System prevents creation of duplicate email addresses
- Import processes include duplicate detection

**Merge Strategies**:
- Identify potential duplicates during import
- Manual merge process for confirmed duplicates
- Preserve all relationship data during merges

### Data Validation

**Email Validation**:
- Format checking for valid email structure
- Domain validation for business email requirements
- Blacklist support for excluding certain domains

**Phone Number Validation**:
- Format standardization for consistent display
- International number support
- Extension handling for business numbers

**Required Field Enforcement**:
- Ensure critical information is always captured
- Configurable requirements based on business needs
- Validation messages guide proper data entry

- Use consistent job title terminology
- Maintain uniform address formats

**Complete Information**:
- Always include email addresses when available
- Link people to companies whenever possible
- Add job titles for professional context
- Include phone numbers for direct contact

### Relationship Management

**Company Associations**:
- Always link people to their companies
- Keep company information up to date
- Use company relationships for account management
- Track job changes and company moves

**Activity Tracking**:
- Log important conversations and meetings
- Create follow-up tasks for future actions
- Use notes to capture relationship context
- Track communication preferences and patterns

### Privacy and Compliance

**Data Protection**:
- Respect privacy preferences and opt-out requests
- Implement data retention policies
- Secure sensitive personal information

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Email Sync Problems

**Missing Emails**:
- Check email visibility settings
- Verify person's email address is correct
- Ensure email folders are included in sync
- Confirm email provider integration is active

**Duplicate People**:
- Review email address variations
- Check for typos in email addresses
- Use merge functionality for confirmed duplicates
- Implement validation rules to prevent future duplicates

### Data Quality Issues

**Incomplete Information**:
- Set up validation rules for required fields
- Create workflows to prompt for missing information
- Regular data quality audits and cleanup
- Training for consistent data entry practices

**Relationship Problems**:
- Verify company records exist before linking
- Check for correct company name spelling
- Update relationships when people change jobs
- Maintain current employment information
