## Why Connect Your Calendar?

Connecting your calendar to Twenty automatically captures meeting information and links it to your CRM records. This gives you a complete view of all customer interactions without manual data entry.

### What Gets Synced
- **Meeting details**: Title, date, time, and location
- **Attendee information**: Automatically linked to People records
- **Meeting history**: Complete timeline of interactions
- **Future meetings**: Upcoming appointments and their context

## Supported Calendar Providers

### Google Calendar
- **Full integration** with Google Workspace accounts
- **OAuth authentication** for secure connection
- **Real-time sync** of meeting updates
- **Attendee mapping** to existing contacts

### Outlook Calendar
- **Microsoft 365** and Outlook.com support
- **Exchange integration** for enterprise accounts
- **Automatic sync** of meeting details
- **Contact linking** with CRM records

### CalDAV (Advanced)
- **Any CalDAV-compatible** calendar service
- **Custom server** configuration
- **Manual setup** required
- **Technical knowledge** needed

## Connecting Google Calendar

### Step 1: Access Account Settings
1. Go to **Settings** in the left sidebar
2. Click **Accounts** under the Workspace section
3. Look for Google Calendar connection options

### Step 2: Authorize Google Access
1. Click **Connect Google Calendar**
2. Sign in with your Google account
3. Grant Twenty permission to access your calendar
4. You'll be redirected back to Twenty

### Step 3: Configure Sync Settings
After connecting, you can set:
- **Sync range**: How far back and forward to sync
- **Calendar selection**: Which calendars to include
- **Meeting visibility**: Which meetings appear in Twenty
- **Attendee linking**: How to handle unknown attendees

## What Happens After Connection

### Automatic Meeting Import
- **Past meetings** are imported based on your sync range
- **Future meetings** sync automatically
- **Meeting updates** are reflected in real-time
- **Cancellations** are updated in Twenty

### Attendee Linking
Twenty automatically:
1. **Checks for existing People records** using email addresses
2. **Creates new People records** for unknown attendees (if enabled)
3. **Links meetings** to all attendee records
4. **Associates with Companies** based on attendee relationships

### Meeting Timeline
Meetings appear in:
- **People records**: All meetings with that contact
- **Company records**: Meetings with company employees
- **Opportunity records**: Meetings related to deals
- **Global timeline**: Complete meeting history

## Managing Calendar Data

### Viewing Meeting History
1. Open any Person or Company record
2. Click the **Calendar** tab
3. Browse through meeting history
4. Click meetings to see full details

### Adding Meeting Notes
After meetings, you can:
1. Open the meeting record in Twenty
2. Add notes about what was discussed
3. Create follow-up tasks
4. Link to relevant opportunities or projects

## Troubleshooting Calendar Sync

### Sync Not Working
**Check these common issues**:
- Verify calendar connection is still active
- Ensure calendar permissions haven't changed
- Check internet connection and Twenty status
- Look for calendar provider service issues

### Missing Meetings
**Possible causes**:
- Meeting is outside sync date range
- Calendar is not included in sync settings
- Meeting was marked as private
- Attendee emails don't match People records

## Best Practices

### Meeting Management
- **Use descriptive titles** for better context in Twenty
- **Include relevant attendees** to ensure proper linking
- **Add meeting locations** for better record keeping
- **Update meeting details** when plans change

### CRM Integration
- **Review synced meetings** regularly for accuracy
- **Add meeting notes** for important discussions
- **Create follow-up tasks** from meeting outcomes
- **Link meetings** to relevant opportunities
